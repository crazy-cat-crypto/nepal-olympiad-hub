International Physics Olympiad (IPhO)

Challenging minds through experimental and theoretical physics since 1967

About IPhO

The International Physics Olympiad (IPhO) is an annual physics competition for high school students. It is one of the International Science Olympiads. The first IPhO was held in Warsaw, Poland in 1967.

Each national delegation consists of at most five student competitors plus two leaders, who are usually physics teachers from their country. The students compete individually, but there is an unofficial country ranking. The competition consists of two parts - theoretical and experimental, both taking 5 hours each.

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be enrolled in secondary education (typically under 20 years old)
  • Education Level: Cannot have started university studies in physics or related fields
  • Selection Process: Through national physics olympiad conducted in Nepal
  • Training: Intensive theoretical and experimental training for selected students
  • Team Size: Nepal sends up to 5 students annually
