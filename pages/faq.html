Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Olympiads in Nepal

What are International Olympiads?‚ñº
International Olympiads are annual academic competitions for high school students in subjects like Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Informatics, Astronomy, and Linguistics. They test problem-solving skills and deep understanding beyond regular curriculum.
Am I eligible to participate?‚ñº
Generally, you must be: (1) Under 20 years old, (2) Currently in secondary/high school, (3) Not enrolled in university. Specific age limits vary by olympiad. Most importantly, you don't need to be a genius - with dedication and proper preparation, any motivated student can compete!
How much does it cost?‚ñº
Registration for national olympiads typically costs NPR 200-500. If selected for international competitions, the Nepal team usually covers travel, accommodation, and competition fees. Some organizations offer scholarships for training materials.
Do I need prior olympiad experience?‚ñº
No! Everyone starts somewhere. Begin with national-level competitions to gain experience. Many successful olympiad participants started in Grade 9 or 10 with zero experience. The key is consistent practice and learning from each attempt.
How do I register for olympiads in Nepal?‚ñº
Contact the organizing bodies: MAN for Math (man.org.np), NASO for Astronomy (naso.org.np), NAPTEC for Physics, or your school's science department. Registration typically opens in September-October. Follow their Facebook pages for announcements.
What's the selection process?‚ñº
Step 1: National Olympiad (December-February) ‚Üí Step 2: Top performers selected for training ‚Üí Step 3: Final selection exam ‚Üí Step 4: Intensive training camp ‚Üí Step 5: International competition (July-August). Total process takes about 8-10 months.
How should I prepare?‚ñº
Start early! (1) Master school curriculum first, (2) Solve past papers, (3) Use recommended books (listed on olympiad pages), (4) Join study groups or online communities, (5) Practice regularly - at least 1-2 hours daily. Consistency beats cramming!
Can olympiad success help with university admissions?‚ñº
Absolutely! Olympiad medals and participation are highly valued by universities worldwide, especially MIT, Caltech, IITs, and top Asian/European universities. They demonstrate problem-solving ability, dedication, and academic excellence beyond grades.
What if I don't win a medal?‚ñº
Participation itself is valuable! You'll develop problem-solving skills, meet brilliant peers, learn advanced topics, and build discipline. Many olympiad participants go on to top universities even without medals. The journey matters more than the destination.
Can I participate in multiple olympiads?‚ñº
Yes! Many students participate in 2-3 olympiads. Math and Physics complement each other well. However, focus on quality over quantity - it's better to prepare deeply for one olympiad than superficially for many.
Is online training available?‚ñº
Yes! Resources include: AoPS (Math), Codeforces (Informatics), PhysicsOlympiad.tech, Khan Academy, and YouTube channels. Join Nepal Olympiad Facebook groups for peer learning and mentorship from past participants.
When should I start preparing?‚ñº
Ideally, Grade 9-10 to build foundation. But it's never too late! Grade 11 students can still compete successfully with focused preparation. Start NOW - download past papers, join communities, and practice daily.
