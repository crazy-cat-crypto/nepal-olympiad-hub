Exploring the cosmos through theoretical, observational, and data analysis challenges

About IOAA

The International Olympiad on Astronomy and Astrophysics (IOAA) is an annual astronomy competition for high school students. It is one of the International Science Olympiads. The competition involves theoretical, practical (observational and data analysis), and team components.

Nepal has a strong tradition in astronomy education and has been participating in international astronomy competitions through the Nepal Astronomical Society (NASO) and Nepal Association for Astronomy and Space Science Olympiad (NAASO).

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be in secondary education (typically under 18 years old)
  • Education Level: Not enrolled in university during the competition year
  • Selection Process: Through National Astronomy Olympiad conducted by NASO/NAASO
  • Training: Theoretical, observational, and data analysis training provided
  • Team Size: Nepal sends 5 students annually

Competition Components

Theoretical

Celestial mechanics, stellar physics, cosmology, coordinate systems

Observational

Star identification, telescope usage, celestial coordinate measurement

Data Analysis

Photometry, spectroscopy, image analysis, astronomical calculations

Planetarium

Sky recognition, constellation identification, celestial phenomena

Team Round

Collaborative problem-solving in astronomy and astrophysics

Mathematics

Spherical trigonometry, calculus, coordinate transformations
