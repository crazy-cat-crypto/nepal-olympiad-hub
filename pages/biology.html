International Biology Olympiad (IBO)

Exploring life sciences through theoretical and practical challenges since 1990

About IBO

The International Biology Olympiad (IBO) is an annual biology competition for high school students. It is one of the International Science Olympiads. The first IBO was held in Czechoslovakia in 1990.

Each country sends a team of four students who compete individually. The competition consists of theoretical and practical examinations covering all areas of biology, from molecular and cellular biology to ecology and evolution.

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be in secondary education (typically under 20 years old)
  • Education Level: Cannot have started university studies
  • Selection Process: Through national biology olympiad conducted in Nepal
  • Training: Theoretical and laboratory training covering diverse biological topics
  • Team Size: Nepal sends 4 students annually

Contact Information

Organization: Nepal Academy of Science and Technology (NAST)

Alternative: Contact through school biology departments

Research Opportunities

IBO medalists gain direct access to cutting-edge biological research at leading universities and research institutes. Many participants receive positions in genetics labs, neuroscience centers, and conservation biology programs studying everything from molecular biology to ecology.

Medical School Advantage

IBO success guarantees admission to top medical schools and life science programs (Harvard Medical, Johns Hopkins, Cambridge, Imperial College) with full scholarships. Medical schools and biology departments worldwide actively recruit IBO medalists.

Biotech & Healthcare Careers

Master molecular biology, genetics, physiology, and ecological problem-solving skills essential for careers in biotechnology, pharmaceutical research, and healthcare innovation. These skills are directly applicable to genetic engineering, drug development, and personalized medicine.

Global Biology Network

Connect with brilliant young biologists from 75+ countries. Many IBO alumni become leading researchers in genomics, neuroscience, and work at institutions like WHO, NIH, biotech companies like Moderna, Illumina, and conservation organizations shaping the future of life on Earth.
