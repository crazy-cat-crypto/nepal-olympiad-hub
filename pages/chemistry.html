International Chemistry Olympiad (IChO)

Challenging students in theoretical and practical chemistry since 1968

About IChO

The International Chemistry Olympiad (IChO) is an annual academic competition for high school students. It is one of the International Science Olympiads. The first IChO was held in Prague, Czechoslovakia, in 1968.

Each country can send a team of up to 4 students, who compete individually. The competition consists of theoretical and practical examinations, testing students' knowledge and skills in various areas of chemistry.

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be in secondary education (typically under 20 years old)
  • Education Level: Cannot have started university studies in chemistry or related fields
  • Selection Process: Through national chemistry olympiad conducted in Nepal
  • Training: Intensive theoretical and laboratory training for selected students
  • Team Size: Nepal sends up to 4 students annually

Research Opportunities

IChO medalists gain access to cutting-edge chemistry research at top laboratories and pharmaceutical companies. Many participants receive research positions at leading institutions studying drug discovery, materials science, and sustainable chemistry.
