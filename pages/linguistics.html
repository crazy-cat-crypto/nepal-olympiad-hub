International Linguistics Olympiad (IOL)

Solving language puzzles and exploring the science of human language

About IOL

The International Linguistics Olympiad (IOL) is an annual competition for high school students interested in language and linguistics. The first IOL was held in 2003 in Bulgaria.

Unlike language proficiency tests, IOL focuses on linguistic problem-solving. Students analyze unfamiliar languages, decipher writing systems, and apply logical reasoning to language puzzles - no prior knowledge of linguistics required!

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be in secondary education
  • Prerequisites: No prior linguistics knowledge required - only logical thinking!
  • Selection Process: Through national olympiads or direct participation
  • Skills Tested: Pattern recognition, logical reasoning, analytical thinking
  • Team Size: Teams of 4 students (individual and team contests)

Competition Structure

IOL consists of two rounds: an individual contest where students solve 5 linguistic puzzles independently, and a team contest where groups of 4 collaborate on one complex problem. Problems involve analyzing unfamiliar languages, deciphering patterns, and applying logical reasoning.
