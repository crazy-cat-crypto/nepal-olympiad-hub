International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI)

The premier algorithmic programming competition for secondary school students

About IOI

The International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI) is an annual competitive programming competition for secondary school students. It is one of the five international science olympiads. The first IOI was held in 1989 in Bulgaria.

Students compete individually by solving algorithmic programming problems using languages like C, C++, Java, or Python. The competition tests skills in algorithms, data structures, and efficient problem-solving.

Eligibility & Selection

  • Age Requirement: Must be in secondary education (typically under 20 years old)
  • Education Level: Not enrolled in university
  • Selection Process: Through national informatics olympiad and programming competitions
  • Training: Algorithmic problem-solving, data structures, competitive programming
  • Team Size: Nepal sends up to 4 students annually

Contact Information

National Competitions: Various programming competitions organized by universities and tech organizations

Competition Structure

The IOI consists of two competition days, each with a 5-hour session. Students solve 3 algorithmic problems per day, implementing solutions that are automatically evaluated on test cases.
